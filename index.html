About Us

One World Analytics was founded in 2013 as a research company that uses innovative approaches to the analysis of global social and environmental issues. We use a combination of text mining, statistics, geographic mapping and network analysis to make the invisible and the neglected visible.

We have a strong commitment to working with developing countries to address knowledge and capacity needs that will contribute to economic development and conservation of the environment.

Services

One World Analytics focuses on supporting developing countries in implementing the UN Convention on Biological Diversity and its Nagoya Protocol and conducts wider work across the spectrum of global environmental issues, including the Plant Treaty, Marine biodiversity and intellectual property at WIPO.

Design

We created a model for integrated national research permit systems to support implementation of the CBD and its Nagoya Protocol by participating governments. The model has been implemented in The Bahamas and Kenya and work is ongoing in other countries in Africa. The development and implementation of the model in The Bahamas is supported by UNEP-GEF and in African countries by the ABS Initiative coordinated by GIZ. The concept documents for the model are openly available at Abs Permit System

Research

We conduct specialist research on trends in science and technology involving biodiversity. Research is typically focused on informing international policy debates. Former clients for research include the UK government, the European Commission, the World Intellectual Property Organisation (WIPO), the Plant Treaty under the UN Food and Agriculture Organisation, and the German Development Cooperation (GIZ). We are committed to open access and normally only conduct research that can be made publicly available free of charge.

Monitoring

We have developed a machine learning based monitoring system for scientific research and patent activity involving biodiversity. The system focuses on extracting biodiversity information from hundreds of millions of texts and presenting the results for decision-makers in interactive form.

Training

Members of staff provide training in patent analytics in developing countries as part of work with the Wipo

PROJECTS

We have worked on a number of projects

Africa:

We are presently working with partner organisations to support implementation of research permit and monitoring systems in Cameroon, Kenya, Madagascar, Namibia and South Africa. In conducting this project we work in partnerships with Geomedia Germany and the ABS Capacity Development Initiativecoordinated byGIZ.

Caribbean

We are working on the implementation of the National research permit system in The Bahamas" and in discussion on designing similar projects elsewhere in the Caribbean.

Global

We have conducted research on the topic of digital sequence information commissioned by the European Commission as a contribution to international policy debates on options to address this topic under the Biodiversity Convention and a range of other policy processes.


Intellectual Property

We are committed to advancing methods to make patent analytics accessible to all. In work with the World Intellectual Property Organisation (WIPO) our staff have developed the WIPO Manual on Open Source Patent Analytics and the WIPO Handbook on Patent Analytics". Work patent analytics provides the basis for training workshops with developing countries held in Africa, Asia, South America.

India

We designed the national monitoring system for genetic resources in India in partnership with the Centre for Development of Advanced Computingin Chennai in support of the National Biodiversity Authority". The project was completed in 2019 and supported by GIZ India.

Marine

We conducted ground breaking research on scientific and patent activity in the deep oceans for the UK to inform debates on a new treaty on marine biodiversity under the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea.

PARTNERSHIPS

One World Analytics works in close partnership with Amband Ltd in Kenya and complements our analytics skills with expertise in system development. Amband is a Nairobi based IT company with a strong track record in programming and delivery and maintenance of operational systems. The data science and machine learning team is a joint endeavour between One World Analytics and Amband with staff based in the UK and Kenya.
